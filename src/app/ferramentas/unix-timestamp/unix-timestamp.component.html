What is Unix Timestamp

Unix Timestamp is a numeric representation of time, usually expressed in seconds since midnight (00:00:00) on January 1, 1970, in UTC (Coordinated Universal Time).

Its origin dates back to the development of the Unix operating system in the 1970s. At that time, programmers needed a standard way to represent dates and times that was independent of time zones and easy to manipulate computationally.

The creators of Unix, mainly Ken Thompson and Dennis Ritchie, adopted a time system based on the number of seconds elapsed since a fixed reference date. This choice provided simplicity and consistency when handling dates and times, regardless of the computer or user's geographic location.

Unix Timestamp is therefore a universal and consistent way to represent time, widely used in computer systems to record and manipulate dates and times accurately and efficiently.
